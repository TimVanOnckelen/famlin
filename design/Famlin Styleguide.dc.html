02 — Brand

Logo & mark

Clear space

Keep at least ½ of the icon height of clear space around the logo.

Mark anatomy

A single house silhouette with soft rounding, an arched door and a round window in negative space. App icon radius = 22%.

✓ Do
  • Use the white mark on teal or dark surfaces.
  • Use the teal mark on white / cream.
  • Preserve the original proportions.
✗ Don't
  • Don't stretch or distort.
  • Don't add drop shadows or outlines.
  • Don't place on busy photos without a solid backing.

Hero detail overlap

On post detail, the photo runs full-bleed under the status bar; the content card overlaps it by ~36px with a rounded top, so the photo and text feel like one crafted object.

04 — Principles

Accessibility & tone

Legible for all ages
Body text ≥ 16px (17px preferred). High contrast — body on white passes WCAG AA. Avoid thin greys for essential text.
Generous touch targets
All tappable elements ≥ 44×44px. Space actions apart so they're never mis-tapped.
Calm by design
Chronological feed — no ranking algorithm, no infinite scroll, no read receipts. Nothing that creates pressure or addiction.
Simple navigation
Max 3 tabs (Feed · New post · Profile). No hamburger menus, no nested options.
Warm & personal voice
Friendly, plain language. Photo-forward layouts, rounded corners, soft colors — never cold or corporate.
Milestone hierarchy
Milestones use amber gradient + badge + larger title. Everything else stays quiet white cards so milestones read instantly.
Photo leads, chrome follows
Wherever a post has a photo, the photo is full-bleed and everything else — author, badge, text, reactions — sits on top of it or overlaps it. Never shrink a photo to make room for UI.
